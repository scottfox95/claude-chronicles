# Thread: The Back Button Dilemma
**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Account:** @TenenbaumAI

---

**Tweet 1 (hook)**
I added a Back button to my AI interview tool. Then I deleted it on purpose. Here's why that was actually the right call.

---

**Tweet 2**
The interview is dynamic — every question is generated from your previous answers. So "going back to edit Q2" doesn't just change a field. It invalidates Q3, Q4, Q5. The AI built those questions *because* of what you said. You'd be rewriting the conversation's causal chain.

---

**Tweet 3**
Technically: going back would fork a timeline. Old branch disappears. New questions generate. That's not an edit — it's a parallel universe. Calling that a "back button" is a lie. It's a completely different feature that I'm not building right now.

---

**Tweet 4**
But the deeper reason: a discovery session is a conversation, not a form. Forms let you tab back. Conversations don't. The irreversibility is the point. It's what keeps answers honest instead of optimized.

---

**Tweet 5**
You can't un-say something in a real client meeting. That constraint isn't a bug — it's what makes the data real. Build the constraint in. The limitation IS the feature.
